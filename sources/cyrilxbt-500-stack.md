Argues for replacing a 9-app stack (Notion/Roam/Evernote/Todoist/Bear/Readwise/
etc.) with one Obsidian vault: own your plain-markdown files, link instead of
nest, and run three workflows. Same 4-folder minimalism (00 Inbox / 01 Notes /
02 Projects / 03 Resources), 5 plugins (Templater, Dataview, **Tasks**, QuickAdd,
Obsidian Git), evergreen/daily/project templates.

## The keystone claim (most relevant to "a workflow that covers everything")

**The thing that "covers everything" is not automation — it is the weekly review.**

> "The one thing that makes or breaks this system: the weekly review. Everything
> else is infrastructure. The weekly review is the habit that makes the
> infrastructure pay off." (30 min/week: Clear → Review → Plan; incl. "open 3
> random notes" for serendipitous resurfacing.)

And, again, the anti-everything message:
- "Most people build a vault that tries to do everything — and ends up doing
  nothing well... a system for a version of themselves that doesn't exist."
- "Does this make capturing faster, processing easier, or surfacing more likely?
  If not, skip it."
- "The vault that works is not the most complex one. It's the one you actually use."
- "Use the system for 30 days before adding anything else."

## What this means for MyCVHVault

- The "workflow that covers everything" Christophe is reaching for is a **ritual
  (the weekly review)**, not a machine. The agent's job is to make capture,
  surfacing, and the safety net cheaper — not to replace the review.
- Reinforces `H6` (Obsidian Git as backup/rollback) — flagged as essential in
  every one of these guides.
- Christophe's edge: he is already past the 4-folder beginner stage and has
  `04 Decisions`. His failure mode is over-building; the discipline he needs is
  the 30-day / "real limitation" test, plus the weekly review habit.
