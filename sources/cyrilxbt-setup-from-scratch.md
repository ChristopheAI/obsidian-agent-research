# Source Note — CyrilXBT "Set Up Obsidian From Scratch (works for your brain)"

- URL: https://x.com/cyrilXBT/status/2061970056628560271
- Date: 2026-06-03 (**111K views** — his most-viewed Obsidian piece)
- Source grade: **B for this artifact** (concrete, sober), promotional account (C).

A beginner setup guide preaching radical minimalism: inbox-first capture, a
brutal 4-folder structure (00 Inbox / 01 Notes / 02 Projects / 03 Resources), a
simple note format with a `Related` field, the daily note as a working surface,
**only 5 plugins** (Templater, Dataview, Calendar, QuickAdd, Obsidian Git), a
20-minute weekly review, and retrieval-first naming.

## The core message (directly relevant to "how to build a workflow that covers everything")

This guide is the antidote to "covers everything":

- "Most people built a system for an imaginary version of themselves."
- "You need 4 folders, not 15. Do not add more until you've used this 30 days
  and hit a real limitation."
- "Add plugins only when you hit a real problem — not problems you imagine."
- "The goal is NOT to capture everything. It's to think better."
- "Obsidian is not a storage system. It's a thinking environment."
- "Let it grow only as fast as your actual needs demand."

## The productive contradiction (a real finding)

Same author, two opposite philosophies, three days apart:

- This piece (Jun 3, **111K views**): keep it brutally simple; AI is not even
  mentioned; "don't capture everything; think better."
- `cyrilxbt-second-brain-guide.md` (Jun 6, 39K views): an elaborate 8-folder,
  6-Claude-integration "system that thinks."

The simpler message got ~3x the reach. Takeaway: this is content optimized for
engagement, not a coherent doctrine — but the **simple pole is the correct one**,
and it independently confirms our own conclusions and kepano's "writing is
thinking." When the same influencer's minimalist post outperforms his
maximalist post 3:1, that is the audience (and reality) voting.
